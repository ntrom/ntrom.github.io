Academic Projects

Here you'll find some of the projects I developed during my time at Stony Brook University.

Lotus Field

Made with: C# XNA (ported to MonoGame)

Lotus Field is a game project developed by myself, two other programmers, and an artist. It was a game that used concepts of music to represent harmony and dissonance within a colony of underwater creatures.

Volume Revolution

Made with: C++ DirectX

Volume Revolution is a game project developed by myself, and two other programmers. I came up with the idea after years of playing guitar and always feeling like I had to worry about the volume. The main character is modeled after myself, and the objective of the game is to make it through the levels using your guitar to blast soundwaves at enemies and obstacles. Each level has a musical key signature, made of color-coded chords that the player collects to get through the level. This project was a winner of the annual SBU Game Programming Competition.

Corey Conundrum

Made with: C# Unity Blender

Corey Conundrum is a 3D game project about drums. I built this project myself, focusing heavily on learning the Unity game engine.
